1. Introduction to Sales Forecasting

Sales forecasting is estimating future sales revenue. A forecast predicts what WILL happen; a target defines what you WANT to happen.

Why It Matters
  • Resource allocation & territory sizing
  • Inventory & supply chain readiness
  • Hiring, training & development budgets
  • Credible, committable targets
  • Early gap identification
  • Leadership credibility through data-driven planning

2. Quantitative Forecasting Methods

SMA = (Sum of last N periods) ÷ N

Smooths short-term fluctuations. Best for stable markets, mature products, as a baseline anchor.

Limitations: Lags behind rapid changes, treats all periods equally, choice of N matters greatly.

Forecast = Target MS% × Total Market Size

Powerful in pharma because IQVIA provides reliable total market data. Use Geo-Share to compare each territory's market size as a % of total — territories with high geo-share but low MS are your biggest opportunities.

  • Declining MS: Defensive strategy before aggressive targets
  • Low MS in large market: Untapped potential, needs capability building
  • High MS in small market: Limited growth ceiling
  • Competitor >40%: Targeted displacement, not just more calls

Forecast = α × Actual + (1-α) × Previous Forecast

α near 1 = responsive but noisy. α near 0 = smooth but slow. Pharma typical: 0.2-0.4. Key concept: giving more weight to recent data is often more accurate. Many pharma tools (IQVIA Forecaster) use this behind the scenes.

3. Qualitative Forecasting Methods

Aggregates individual rep forecasts. Captures local intelligence no database provides.

Process

  1. Each rep submits territory forecast from customer knowledge
  2. Manager reviews, challenges, adjusts based on historical accuracy
  3. Regional aggregation for district forecast
  4. Management adjustment for strategic factors

Common Pitfalls

  • Sandbagging: Under-forecasting for easier targets
  • Optimism bias: New reps over-forecast
  • Recency bias: Great Q1 → linear extrapolation
  • Anchoring: Stuck on last year's numbers

Structured anonymous expert rounds. Best for new launches, regulatory impact, competitor entry, long-range (3-5yr) planning.

  1. Round 1: Independent forecasts with rationale
  2. Round 2: Anonymous results shared; experts revise
  3. Round 3: Convergence; outliers explain reasoning
  4. Facilitator synthesizes consensus with range

4-5. Combining Methods & Target Distribution

No single method is sufficient. Use 2+ and compare:

MethodForecastBasis
Moving Average30M3-year average
Market Share33MCurrent MS × market
Company Target42MManagement ambition
Growth-Based37M×1.15

Always present 3 scenarios: Best Case / Most Likely / Worst Case. Single-point forecasts create false confidence.

SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Every target must pass this test.

Distribution Methods

  • Equal: Same target per territory. Risk: ignores differences.
  • Historical: Based on past contribution. Risk: locks in inequities.
  • Market Potential: Based on geo-share. Risk: ignores current capability.
  • Hybrid (recommended): Weighted combination.
    Formula: Target = (W1×Historical% + W2×Potential%) × Total

Weights: 60/40 historical/potential for mature products. 40/60 for growth products.

6-8. CRM Data, Limitations & Technology

MetricTells YouImplication
Coverage %Doctors visitedLow = unfulfilled potential
FrequencyVisits per doctorBelow benchmark = weak voice
Daily CallsProductivityLow = capacity/motivation
A-Class FocusEffort allocationMisallocated = low ROI

Reading the Patterns

  • Low coverage + low frequency: Activity problem → coverage plan first
  • High activity + flat sales: Effectiveness problem → skills training
  • Coverage >100%: Calling non-potential → list optimization
  • Solid metrics + declining MS: Competitive pressure → investigate

Challenges: Incomplete IQVIA coverage, 6-8 week reporting lag, hospital data gaps, no history for new products, inconsistent CRM entry.

Workarounds: Proxy indicators, Sales Force Composite, multiple methods (3+ when data is weak), document all assumptions, sensitivity analysis (±20%).

  • Excel/Sheets: Pivot tables, FORECAST function, scenarios
  • IQVIA Forecaster: Industry-standard market-level
  • Veeva CRM Analytics: Territory-level insights
  • Power BI / Tableau: Trend visualization
  • AI/ML: Emerging, best at national/regional level
Note: Technology augments but never replaces your judgment.

10-11. Key Takeaways & Assignment

Seven Principles for Forecasting Excellence
  1. Use available data effectively
  2. Apply appropriate methods per situation
  3. Combine methods (MA + MS + Sales Force Composite)
  4. Present scenarios (best/likely/worst)
  5. SMART framework + cascade with rationale
  6. Leverage team ground-level knowledge
  7. Refine quarterly: forecast vs. actual
